# Strategic Analysis

Four frameworks for analyzing business strategy. Apply in order for a comprehensive view.

---

## A. SWOT Analysis

Strengths (internal positive), Weaknesses (internal negative), Opportunities (external positive), Threats (external negative).

- Base on specific evidence, not generic statements
- Cross-reference: do strengths map to opportunities? Do weaknesses expose threats?
- Prioritize: which 2-3 items in each quadrant matter most?

## B. PESTLE Analysis

Macro-environmental factors:
- **Political**: tax policy, trade restrictions, regulatory stability, government stability
- **Economic**: inflation, interest rates, GDP growth, unemployment, disposable income
- **Social**: demographics, lifestyle trends, education levels, cultural attitudes
- **Technological**: automation, R&D activity, tech adoption rates, innovation pace
- **Legal**: employment law, consumer protection, health/safety regulations, antitrust
- **Environmental**: climate policy, sustainability requirements, carbon regulations

Focus on factors that directly impact the business or industry. Not every factor matters.

## C. Porter's Five Forces

Industry structure analysis:
1. **Threat of new entrants**: barriers to entry (capital, regulation, brand, network effects)
2. **Bargaining power of suppliers**: concentration, switching costs, forward integration threat
3. **Bargaining power of buyers**: concentration, price sensitivity, backward integration threat
4. **Threat of substitutes**: alternatives, price-performance tradeoff, switching costs
5. **Rivalry among existing competitors**: concentration, growth rate, differentiation, exit barriers

Score each force (low/medium/high) and assess overall industry attractiveness.

## D. Growth Loops

Identify sustainable growth mechanisms (flywheels):
1. Map the current growth model: acquisition, activation, retention, revenue, referral
2. Identify reinforcing loops: how does X cause Y which increases X?
3. Quantify: what's the cycle time? What's the amplification factor?
4. Identify friction: what's slowing the loop? What would accelerate it?

Example loops: content SEO loop (more content → more traffic → more backlinks → better rankings → more traffic), viral loop (more users → more invites → more users), marketplace loop (more buyers → more sellers → better selection → more buyers).
